Saigon Noodle Salad

Description

Saigon Noodle Salad, what is that many of you are thinking right about now. This is a Vietnamese style noodle dish. This is a great meal to try if you are in a funk with your current dinner options. This meas is bursting with flavor and makes great use of leftover grilled meat or shrimp. This is a quick meal to make with a total of 30 minutes but could be less if you are using some left over meat or shrip from last night BBQ!

Ingredients

dressing

  • 1/4 cup of water, or more to taste
  • 3 tablespoons lime juice
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar, or more to taste
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon minchedfresh ginger root
  • 1/2 teaspoon Sriracha chile sauce

Salad

  • 1 (8 ounce) package (linguine-width) rice noodles
  • 2 cups thinly sliced Napa (Chinese) cabbage
  • 1 1/2 cups matchsick-cut carrots
  • 8 ounces grilled shrip (or leftover shrimp or meat)
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/2 English cucumber, halved lengthwise and cut into thin slices
  • 2 2/3 tablespoons chopped fresh mint
  • 2 2/3 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
  • 1/2 cup coarsely chopped peanuts

Steps

  1. Whish water, lime juice, fish sauce, brown sugar, garlic, ginger, and Sriracha together in a bowl until the sugar is dissolved
  2. Bring a large pot of water to a full boil; remove from the heat and soak the rice noodles in the hot water for one minute.
  3. Stir to separate the noodles and continue soaking until the noodles are tender, this should take about 3 minutes.
  4. Drain the noodles and rinse with cold water until the noodles are cooled.
  5. Shake the noodles in a colander to drain as much water as possible.
  6. Mix noodles, cabbage, carrots, shrimp, bean sprouts, cucumber slices, green onions, mint, cilantro, and basil together in a large bowl.
  7. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss to coat.
  8. Lastly, top the salad with the chopped peanuts
